## Why Clients Ask for Scanned Contracts

You might wonder why clients still request "scanned copies" in a digital world. Here are the common reasons:

### Legal and Compliance Requirements

Many organizations have document retention policies that require scanned copies. Insurance companies, government agencies, and large corporations often have legacy systems designed around physical document workflows.

### Signature Verification

A scanned document with a signature looks more authentic than a plain digital PDF. It signals that the document has been physically handled and personally signed, adding a layer of perceived authenticity.

### Tamper Prevention

Once a document is converted to a scanned image format, it becomes much harder to edit. This provides an extra layer of security against unauthorized modifications.

### Standardized Archiving

Companies often archive all contracts in a consistent scanned format. Having your contract match their existing archive format makes their record-keeping easier.

## Step-by-Step Guide

### Step 1: Add Your Signature

Before applying the scan effect, you'll want to sign the contract. You have several options:

**Option A: Use Look Scanned's Built-in Signature Feature**

Look Scanned includes a powerful signature tool that lets you:
- **Draw your signature** directly on your device using the signature pad
- **Type your name** and choose from various signature fonts
- **Upload an image** of your existing signature

This is the most convenient option since you can sign and scan in one place.

**Option B: Use Your Preferred PDF Tool**

Sign the document using Adobe Acrobat, Preview (Mac), or any PDF editor you're comfortable with, then upload the signed PDF to Look Scanned.

### Step 2: Upload to Look Scanned

Go to [lookscanned.io](https://lookscanned.io) and drag your contract into the upload area. Look Scanned supports:

- PDF files
- Word documents (.docx, .doc)
- Images (JPG, PNG, WebP)

Your file is processed entirely in your browser — nothing is uploaded to any server.

### Step 3: Adjust the Scan Effect

Customize the appearance to make your contract look authentically scanned:

- **Colorspace**: Grayscale gives that classic photocopy look
- **Rotation**: Add 0.5-1° tilt to simulate imperfect paper placement
- **Noise**: Low to medium noise adds realistic scanner grain
- **Blur**: A slight blur softens the digital sharpness
- **Border**: Add subtle page edges for authenticity

### Step 4: Download and Send

Preview your scanned contract, then click download. Your professional scanned PDF is ready to email back to your client.

## FAQ

### Is this legally valid?

Yes. The legal validity of a contract depends on the agreement itself, not how it looks. A scanned-looking PDF is just as valid as a "clean" digital PDF. Many courts and businesses routinely accept digitally signed documents with scan effects applied.

### Can people tell it's not a "real" scan?

Practically speaking, no. Look Scanned adds authentic scanning characteristics — slight rotation, noise, paper texture — that are indistinguishable from actual scanned documents. The output looks exactly like it came from a physical scanner.

### Are my contracts secure?

Absolutely. Look Scanned processes everything locally in your browser. Your contracts are never uploaded to any server. Once you close the browser tab, all data is gone. This makes it one of the most privacy-friendly document tools available — essential for confidential business contracts.

### What if my contract has multiple pages?

Look Scanned handles multi-page documents seamlessly. Each page gets the scan effect applied, and you'll receive a single PDF with all pages included.

### Can I use this on my phone?

Yes! Look Scanned is fully responsive and works on smartphones and tablets. You can create scanned contracts directly from your mobile browser — perfect for signing documents while traveling.
