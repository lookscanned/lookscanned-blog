## Why Convert Office Files to Scanned PDFs?

### Meet Submission Requirements

For long-term archival, scanned-style PDFs provide a consistent, tamper-evident format that's widely accepted across industries.

## Tips for Best Results with Office Files

### Font Display Issues

If fonts appear different after conversion, the original fonts may not be available in the browser. Solutions:

- **Embed fonts** in your Word document before uploading (File → Options → Save → Embed fonts)
- **Export to PDF first** from Word/Excel, then upload the PDF to Look Scanned

### Excel Spreadsheets

For wide Excel spreadsheets:

- Adjust the **print area** in Excel before uploading (Page Layout → Print Area)
- Consider using **landscape orientation**
- For very large tables, split into multiple sheets or pages

### Complex Formatting

If your document has complex layouts, charts, or special formatting:

- Export to PDF from the original Office application first
- Then upload the PDF to Look Scanned for the scan effect

### Recommended Settings by Document Type

| Document Type | Colorspace | Rotation | Noise | Blur |
|--------------|------------|----------|-------|------|
| Contracts & Forms | Grayscale | 0.5-1° | Low | Low |
| Reports & Letters | Grayscale | 0.3-0.5° | Medium | Low |
| Spreadsheets | Color | 0.3° | Low | Very Low |
| Presentations | Color | 0.5° | Low | Low |
